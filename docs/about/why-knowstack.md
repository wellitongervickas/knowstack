# Why KnowStack?

What makes KnowStack different from other documentation tools, and why it exists as an open-source project.

## The Problem

Development teams face three recurring challenges with documentation:

- **Fragmented knowledge** — Documentation scattered across wikis, repos, messaging apps, and personal notes
- **Context switching** — Developers constantly leave their editor to search for specs and patterns
- **Cross-team drift** — Frontend and backend teams work with stale or inconsistent information

## What Makes KnowStack Different

**Protocol-native integration**: Connects directly to developer tools through standard protocols (MCP), eliminating context switching — unlike browser-based documentation platforms.

**Bidirectional synchronization**: Changes in the platform reflect in connected repositories and vice versa — not just one-way document reading.

**Living documentation**: Automatically detects when documentation diverges from reality through AI analysis and suggests updates.

**Cross-functional context**: Designed specifically for enabling frontend/backend/ops teams to share knowledge through AI interfaces.

**Developer-first experience**: Built by developers for developers — no training required, works where developers already work.

## Why Open Source

**Self-hostable**: Run KnowStack on your own infrastructure. Your documentation never leaves your network unless you choose to use external AI providers.

**Privacy-first**: No telemetry, no data collection, no vendor lock-in. You own your data completely.

**Transparent security**: Every security claim is verifiable against the source code. No black boxes.

**Community-driven**: Features are driven by real developer needs, not sales targets.
